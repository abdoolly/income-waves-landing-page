FAQ

How is inventory verified?

Amazon's FBA (Fulfillment by Amazon) system marks the inventory as "received" before any funds are released to the seller. This keeps the process safe for investors and transparent for all.

When do I get paid?

Investors typically receive monthly payouts once sales begin. Full principal and profit share are paid after the seller's sales cycle completes.

Can I exit early?

You can request to exit (liquidate) at two key stages:
1. Before funds are released to the seller: You can cancel your investment and get your money back in full, minus any payment processing fees.
2. During the investment cycle (after funds are released): You can request to exit, but your payout will be processed after the current sales cycle completes.
Sales cycles typically last 2â€“6 months, but the exact duration follows each seller's plan.

A small payout fee is taken from each investor payout. There's also a one-time deposit fee to cover payment provider costs.

The platform is being developed with Shariah-compliance in mind. Investments avoid interest (riba), and profit sharing follows a partnership model.

How do I pick a seller or plan?

Each seller presents their Amazon store plan with sales history, product details, and expected profits. You can browse and choose based on your preferences and risk appetite.
